Frequently Asked Questions

Home › FAQs
What is the 4Ps DMIS?
The DMIS for the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps) is an online platform used by DSWD Field Office CAR to manage beneficiary data, compliance monitoring, grievance redress, and reporting for the Cordillera Administrative Region.
What is MC No. 36 and why is it tracked here?
Memorandum Circular No. 36 governs the suspension and delisting of 4Ps beneficiaries who fail to comply with program conditions. The CVS module now includes the consolidated MC No. 36 Validation Result Tracking for P1 2025, showing which households are For MC No. 36 monitoring, qualified for Exemption 1 (beyond their control) or Exemption 2 (system-related issues), or are pending validation.
What do the three MC No. 36 validation results mean?
For MC No. 36 Monitoring: HH failed to comply and is subject to the MC 36 process (Notice of Failure, Evaluation Period, Checkpoint).
Exemption No. 1: HH failed due to reasons beyond their control (e.g., natural calamity, serious illness).
Exemption No. 2: HH failed due to system-related issues (e.g., wrong facility tagging, data mismatch). These require facilitation of updates.
How do I file a grievance through GRS?
Navigate to the GRS module and click "File Grievance." Fill in the complainant's information, nature of grievance, and supporting documents. The system will assign a GRS ticket and route it to the appropriate DMIS officer for action within 30 days.
How do I add my Google Calendar to a module tab?
Go to calendar.google.com → Settings → select your calendar → scroll to "Integrate calendar" → copy the embed URL → paste it in the calendar input field of the relevant module tab, then click Load.
How do I connect a Google Sheet to a module?
One-time setup per module:

1. Open your Google Sheet → click File → Share → Publish to web
2. Select the specific sheet tab (e.g. "ICT Inventory") → format: CSV → click Publish
3. Copy the published URL
4. Go to the relevant module tab (e.g. ICT) → click the green Sync Sheet button
5. Paste the URL → click Connect

After the first connection, clicking Sync Sheet will refresh data instantly. The URL is remembered in your browser. To connect a different sheet tab, add &gid=XXXXXXXXX to the URL (find the gid in the sheet URL after #gid=).
What data should NOT be put in the Google Sheets connected to this dashboard?
Because published Google Sheets are publicly accessible via their CSV URL, do not include any of the following in connected sheets: full names of beneficiaries, employee IDs, email addresses, contact numbers, internal report details, or any personally identifiable information (PII). Only include aggregated statistics, counts, and summary data appropriate for a public-facing dashboard.
