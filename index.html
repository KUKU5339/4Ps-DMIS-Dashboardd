Frequently Asked Questions

Home › FAQs
What is the 4Ps DMIS?
The DMIS for the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps) is an online platform used by DSWD Field Office CAR to manage beneficiary data, compliance monitoring, grievance redress, and reporting for the Cordillera Administrative Region.
Who can access the DMIS dashboard?
Access is granted to authorized DSWD personnel including DMIS Officers, Municipal Links, City/Provincial Coordinators, and supervisors within DSWD FO-CAR. All accounts require multi-factor authentication and role-based access control.
What does NAS/NASH mean in the CVS module?
NAS (Non-Attending Student) and NASH (Non-Attending Student – Health) are beneficiary children who are not attending school or health facilities as required by the 4Ps compliance conditions. The CVS module tracks validated reasons for their non-attendance to enable appropriate interventions.
How do I file a grievance through GRS?
Navigate to the GRS module and click "File Grievance." Fill in the complainant's information, nature of grievance, and supporting documents. The system will assign a GRS ticket and route it to the appropriate DMIS officer for action within 30 days.
How do I add my Google Calendar to a module tab?
Go to calendar.google.com → Settings → select your calendar → scroll to "Integrate calendar" → copy the embed URL → paste it in the calendar input field of the relevant module tab, then click Load.
How does the sidebar work?
The sidebar is collapsed by default to maximize screen space. Simply hover your mouse over the left sidebar to expand it and see all menu labels. Move your mouse away and it will collapse back.
